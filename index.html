How We're Capitalizing
The DR formalized online gambling in 2024 under Resolution 136-2024. With only 4 active licensees and a ~2-year application process, the market is virtually closed to new entrants until 2028. Appuesta's sports betting license is in final-stage approval, expected within 60 days.

At a $4M–$5M valuation, investors are buying into a company valued at just ~1.2% of the addressable market — a modest entry point for a licensed operator with infrastructure already in place.

We are positioned to dominate early market share through:
  • Leones del Escogido partnership — stadium banners, digital billboard, and path to jersey sponsorship activating October 2026 for DR winter baseball league
  • Nationwide media coverage — outdoor advertising secured across Santo Domingo at no cost for 3–6 months
  • Strategic entertainment partnerships — top athletes and sports teams locally onboard
  • Unique payment infrastructure — local DR bank accounts, CARDNET credit cards, and direct bank transfers (exceptionally rare for iGaming operators)

How the SAFE Note Works
A SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is the standard instrument for early-stage raises, created by Y Combinator. The investor provides capital now in exchange for the right to receive equity at a future priced round or exit event, at a price determined by today's agreed valuation cap ($4M–$5M). There are no interest payments, no maturity date, and no board seats required. If Appuesta raises a Series A at $15M, the SAFE investor's capital converts at the lower cap — rewarding them for investing early. Pro-rata rights allow the investor to maintain their ownership percentage in future rounds.
